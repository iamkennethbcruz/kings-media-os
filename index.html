Revenue Infrastructure OS
A complete operating system for running lead generation and email marketing for multiple clients. Follow steps in order when setting up a new client. Use daily to run everything efficiently.
How this system works
Data flows automatically. When you enter subscriber numbers, newsletter stats, or toggle systems, the client status, priority, retainer risk, and weekly wins update on their own. You input the raw numbers. The system does the analysis.
Status is calculated, not guessed. Each client gets a score from 0 to 100 based on system coverage, KPI pass rates, subscriber growth, and newsletter performance. That score sets status automatically.
Follow the step numbers. Steps 01 to 07 are setup and data entry. Step 08 onwards reads that data automatically. Steps 13 and 14 are your daily and weekly operating layer.

Step 02 — Systems live
Toggle each system on or off per client. Click YES or NO to update. All 5 systems must be live before any campaign makes sense to run. System coverage is 30% of the auto-status score.
What each system does — click YES or NO to toggle
Lead magnet: The free resource someone gets when they opt in. Without it there is no reason to give an email address.
DM automation: The ManyChat flow that collects the email and adds it to GHL. This is the entry point for the entire subscriber list.
Newsletter: Weekly email keeping subscribers warm. Without it the list goes cold within 2 to 3 weeks and unsubscribes start climbing.
CRM connected: All subscriber emails flowing into GHL with correct tags. Without this there is no organized list to send to or report on.
Testimonial system: Automated review collection running on autopilot. Social proof is what converts subscribers into buyers.
Step 03 — ManyChat flows
Every automation running per client. Click a client card to expand it and see their flows. Add as many or as few flows as each client actually has. Each client is independent — the page never shows empty space for flows that don't exist.
Flow types and what each one does
DM keyword trigger: Someone sends a specific keyword to the client profile and the flow fires automatically. Highest-volume entry point for subscriber capture.
Comment trigger: Someone comments on a post or Reel with a keyword and receives a DM automatically. Works best on content with strong engagement.
Story reply: Someone replies to a story and a flow starts. Works well for warmer audiences who are already watching content.
Follow trigger: Fires when someone follows the account. Lower intent but builds the list passively.
Lead magnet delivery: Sends the free resource automatically after opt-in. Must be connected to GHL via webhook or subscribers never reach the CRM.
Opt-in rate (target 20%+): Of everyone who started this flow, how many completed it and gave their email. Below 20% means a question is causing drop-off or the flow is too long.

Step 04 — Email subscribers
The primary metric. Update the new subscriber count every Friday. Performance is measured against your own 4-week rolling average — not a goal you set. The baseline comes from your actual results so it cannot be gamed.
What counts as a subscriber and how performance is measured
A subscriber is someone who completed the ManyChat opt-in flow and had their email added to the GHL list. DM conversations alone do not count. Comment triggers alone do not count. Only a completed opt-in with a valid email in GHL counts.
Why a rolling average instead of a goal: A goal you set yourself can be set low to look good. Your 4-week rolling average is calculated from your own actual past results. When your work genuinely improves, the system flags it as above average. When it drops, it shows that too — which is the honest signal that something needs attention.

Step 05 — Newsletter tracker
Every newsletter sent for every client. This is your proof of work and your strongest argument for renewal. When a client asks what results they are getting, this view answers with specific numbers and attributed revenue.
What each metric means and what to do when a number is red
Open rate (target 25%+): Percentage who opened the email. Below 25% means the subject line is not good enough. Rewrite it — try a question, a number, or cut it under 5 words.
Click rate (target 2%+): Percentage who clicked a link inside. Below 2% means the CTA is not specific enough. Tell subscribers exactly what happens when they click.
Skip rate: Percentage who did not open. Auto-calculated from 100 minus open rate. High skip rate over multiple weeks means the list is going cold.
Revenue attributed: Confirmed sales that came directly from this newsletter. Ask the client to confirm after each send. Even one sale per newsletter makes the entire operation worth it to a client.

In nurture: Receiving the email sequence. Opened at least one email.
Responded: Replied, clicked a booking link, or expressed interest.

Step 07 — KPI scores
Weekly numbers per client. Open rate and click rate auto-pull from the most recent newsletter in Step 05. Update opt-in rate and ManyChat completion manually every Friday from ManyChat analytics. These four numbers drive the auto-status in Step 08.
What drives the auto-status score
Client status in Step 08 is calculated from four signals: (1) how many of the 5 systems are live, (2) how many KPIs are passing their target, (3) whether subscriber growth is above or below the 4-week average, and (4) average newsletter open rate. You do not set status manually. You update data and status updates itself.

Step 09 — Retainer health
Contract status and renewal risk per client. Risk level is calculated automatically from the client score and how close they are to renewal. Any client approaching renewal with below-target results needs a proactive conversation now, not when they say they want to cancel.
How risk is calculated automatically
Low risk auto: Client score is 70 or above and renewal is more than 2 months away.
Medium risk auto: Client score is 40 to 69, or renewal is within 2 months with a score below 70.
High risk auto: Client score is below 40, or renewal is within 1 month with a score below 60.
Risk updates automatically when you update KPI data. Override manually using the dropdown if you have context the system cannot see.
Step 10 — Testimonials
Social proof count per client. Update the count weekly. Clients below 5 visible testimonials are losing conversions every day. When a client reaches their goal, a win is auto-logged in Step 12.
What to do based on the count
0 to 3: Critical gap. Send a VideoAsk prompt to recent customers this week. Best prompt: "What was your situation before working with [client], and what specifically changed after?"
4 to 7: Baseline. Keep the system running. Add new ones to Instagram highlights immediately when collected.
8 or more: Good. Now focus on getting video testimonials specifically. Video converts significantly better than written ones.
Step 11 — Split tests
One active test per client at a time. Running two tests at once means you will never know which change worked. Write the hypothesis before making any change. Log the result with specific numbers.
How to run a test correctly
1. Write the hypothesis first: "I believe [change] will improve [metric] because [reason]." Forces you to think before acting.
2. Change exactly one thing: Subject line, CTA wording, opt-in offer, or ManyChat question. One change at a time.
3. Wait at least 7 days: Do not judge a test in 48 hours. You need enough people to go through the flow.
4. Log the result with a specific number: "Open rate went from 24% to 31%" is useful. "It worked" is not.

Step 14 — Daily checklist
Run every morning before starting anything new. Green auto-check means the system has confirmed this is passing from your data. Purple checkbox means you need to manually verify it in the tool.
Rule: fix anything broken before starting any new work
